DILNI ABEYRATHNE ☰

SLEEPY HOLLOW'S FANTASTICAL REALITY

Inspiration for this map came in when I noticed just how different recollections of the same memory or story could be between different people. So, I set about mapping one particular story—Washington Irving’s “The Legend of Sleepy Hollow”—and the differing paths that my friend and I remembered.


Mixing Reality and Fiction


This part of the map explores the spatial relationships between my friend and I’s recollections, overlaid atop a background of the real Sleepy Hollow’s geography and townscape and filled with marks of the imaginary Sleepy Hollow’s landscape (with lots of artistic liberty, of course). In the real Sleepy Hollow realm, a stylized hillshade and sharp, geometric representations of infrastructure are featured. By contrast, the fantasy Sleepy Hollow realm features soft outlines, imperfect brush strokes, and custom-drawn graphics (that headless horseman is my pride and joy).

 

Narrative Tumbles


It’s interesting to see the ways in which my friend and I’s recollections both differed from and matched the true narrative written by Washing Irving. There are plot points both of us forgot, and some both of us added, like how I distinctly remembered Ichabod dying and how my friend distinctly remembered Ichabod escaping into the forest, or how I forgot Ichabod had a lover in Katrina van Tassel and my friend forgot Ichabod was a schoolteacher. They’re factually flawed stories, but no less true to our memories.

Measuring the Memories


Three coincidences! Now, is that good or bad? Neither, of course—this part of the map is dedicated to graphing out the direction our memories went in regarding the legend. The timeline measures (both axes) are taken from the true narrative, and our narratives were plotted within those parameters. Sometimes we were correct, sometimes not, and sometimes we even coincided! But, the accuracy of the narratives doesn’t matter as much as the nature of the memories we had—the pathways of memories can be ever-shifting, but that’s what makes them fun! I mean, how else would I have recalled Ichabod (bravely) battling the undead?
